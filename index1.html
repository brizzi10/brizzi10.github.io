I began as a computer engineering student at Virginia Tech. Despite a great situation, I found the classes very unfulfilling and constantly felt as if I lacked the pasion to pursue the career these studies would eventually manifest. After my sophmore year, an opportunity presented itself to coach a youth basketball team. Fueled by my lifetime passion for the sport, I dove completely into the environment. After the season, I decided to take a break from school to pursue a business training local players.

The success of this venture atracted the attention of a nearby sports comlex, and I was brought in as the Assistant Director of Mens AAU Basketball, and later was promoted to the Head of the Basketball Academy.

Throughout this rewarding period of working with the youth and coaching a sport that I love, never once did I lose my interest in coding. It was the one aspect of my time at Virginia Tech that I enjoyed, and while employed as a coach I also enrolled in Java Classes on the side, and simultaneously began a more cursory study of Web Development.

As my basketball business venture began to reach a plateau, I decided it was time to dive fully into web development, which lead me to General Assembly.
